I am a biochemist by training and a computational biologist by trade, interested in developing and applying computational methods to the study of protein structure, dynamics, and interactions. Throughout my career, I worked on a variety of biological systems ranging from ubiquitin to GPCRs, often in collaboration with experts from different fields. As a result, I managed to retain a firm grasp on biology and biochemistry, and got familiar with data from several different experimental techniques such as NMR, mass spectrometry, FRET, and cryo-EM. 

As of 2020, I have published more than 30 articles in peer-reviewed journals, which have been cited more than 2000 times ([list](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=UeXRdzQAAAAJ)).
