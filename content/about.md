I am a computational biologist interested in the structure and dynamics of protein interactions. I studied biochemistry at the University of Coimbra (Portugal) and later earned my Ph.D. in the lab of [Alexandre Bonvin](https://bonvinlab.org), working on protein interactions and structural bioinformatics. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Stanford University with [Michael Levitt](http://med.stanford.edu/levitt.html).

I am a highly collaborative scientist, having [published more than 30 articles in peer-reviewed journals](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=UeXRdzQAAAAJ), which have received more than 2000 citations. My work has been funded by the [Niels Stensen Fellowship](https://www.nielsstensenfellowship.nl/nl), the [Google Summer of Code program](https://summerofcode.withgoogle.com/), and [The Molecular Sciences Software Institute](https://molssi.org/), among others. As a computational biologist, I spend a significant portion of my research developing software for structural biology, most of which is available for free on my [Github page](https://github.com/joaorodrigues). Some of my most high-profile contributions include being a developer of [HADDOCK](https://wenmr.science.uu.nl/haddock2.4/) and of the [Biopython project](https://biopython.org), as well as the author of [pdb-tools](https://www.bonvinlab.org/pdb-tools). 

Alongside research, I enjoy spending time building and fostering communities in computational biology. I am a certified [Software Carpentry](https://software-carpentry.org/) instructor and I regularly teach Git, Python, and software development best practices to researchers. In addition, I have helped organized several national and international conferences, including an ongoing [EMBO Practical Course](https://meetings.embo.org/event/20-biomolecular-interactions) on modeling protein interactions.
